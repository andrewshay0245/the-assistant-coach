## Practice Structure Sequence

### 1. Prepractice (10-12 min)
- **Low impact stick work** - touches before legs are warm
- Wake up hands, not legs
- Examples: Ping Pong Buddy

### 2. Dynamic Stretch (12 min)
- Get legs ready
- Transition from stick focus to full body

### 3. High Neural Load Work
- **Do this while CNS is fresh** - not when guys are gassed
- Fast-twitch, reaction-based, complex reads
- Brain and neuropathways need to react
- Examples: Oscar Stickwork, 3-on-2's FTS

### 4. Half Field Transition
- Odd numbers: 3v2, 5v4
- Transition concepts in controlled space

### 5. Small Part Breakdown
- 2v2, 4v4
- Build understanding before going full

### 6. Alternate Full Field ↔ Half Field
- Back and forth between full and half field work
- Build up to team concepts

### 7. Ending
- **Always mixing it up** - no predictable ending
- Keeps players locked in, can't mentally check out

## Core Principles

### Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Meter
**Balance process (intrinsic) with scoreboard (extrinsic)**

- **Intrinsic** = infinite game thinking, "am I getting better?" No scoreboard.
- **Extrinsic** = finite game, scoreboard, clear winner/loser

**The balance:**
- Too much scoreboard → deadens the competitive effect
- Too much intrinsic → no accountability, can't measure progress
- **Target ratio: ~4:1** (lean development, keep competition sharp)

### Progression Philosophy
- Individual → Small group → Team
- Simple → Complex
- Half field → Full field → Alternate
